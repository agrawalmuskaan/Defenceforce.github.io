INDIAN NAVY

On December 4th, India celebrates Navy Day to honor the contributions and accomplishments of the Indian Navy to the nation. he Commander-in-Chief of the Indian Navy, the naval component of the Indian Armed Forces, is the President of India. Shivaji, the Maratha Emperor, is regarded as the "Father of the Indian Navy." The Indian Navy plays a significant part in safeguarding the nation's maritime borders and improving India's relations with other countries through visits to seaports, cooperative exercises, disaster assistance, and other activities. In order to strengthen its position in the Indian Ocean region, the modern Indian Navy is undertaking a swift makeover.
The Indian Navy contributes significantly to maintaining the country's maritime borders and advancing India's international relations through a variety of activities such as port visits, collaborations, patriotic missions, disaster assistance, and many more. The modern Indian Navy has undergone changes to strengthen its relationship with the Indian Ocean region. The Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force are the three branches of the Indian Armed Forces. The Indian Army defends us on land, the Navy defends us in the sea, and the Air Force defends us in the air.

The Indian Navy is a well-balanced and cohesive three-dimensional force, capable of operating above, on and under the surface of the oceans, efficiently safeguarding our national interests. The Chief of the Naval Staff (CNS) exercises operational and administrative control of the Indian Navy from the Integrated Headquarters of Ministry of Defence (Navy). He is assisted by the Vice Chief of the Naval Staff (VCNS) and three other Principal Staff Officers, namely the Deputy Chief of the Naval Staff (DCNS), the Chief of Personnel (COP) and the Chief of Material (COM).

The defence of the Andaman & Nicobar Islands is a joint responsibility of all the three services and is coordinated by the Headquarters, Andaman & Nicobar Command, located at PortBlair. This is the only Tri-ServicesCommand in the Indian Armed Forces and is headed by a Commander-in-Chief, as appointed in rotation from the three Services. The Local Naval Defence of the Lakshadweep group of islands is the responsibility of the Naval Officer-in-Charge, Lakshadweep.
